# Summary
The girl, a lover of both storytelling and the mysteries of the lab, found a unique intersection of the two. She would transform her fantastical narratives into tangible realities, using her creations to heal those around her, infusing their suffering with a new vitality.
# Pre-Ascension
Like all dragons, she was abandoned by her parents once she learned to fly. She was never told who her mother or father were—they had long since left her behind. Though this was customary in dragon culture, she never cared about her origins; all she wanted was a way to find happiness.

As a young dragonling, she would often sneak into towns, stealing livestock or hunting wild animals and monsters in the wild. She built her first lair inside a mountain to hide away, but she quickly realized she had a natural talent for magic. Her mother only ever taught her how to use her wings and hunt; she refused to teach her anything else. But over time, she became a master of magic through her own efforts.

Despite her skill, she found it difficult to interact with her kind. They seemed either scarce or hard to find in her world. She was unsure which it was, but she found it lonely. The only other dragon she befriended was a companion she met far away. They grew close, mated occasionally, but neither of their unions produced offspring.

Throughout her long life, she interacted with many races—from humans and elves to dwarves, goblins, and others capable of conversation. She especially liked elves, as they had a deep, remote connection to the earth and lived much longer than other races. She even learned to mimic their appearance using her magic, creating a human form modeled after them.

She didn’t admire how most races lived their lives, though she appreciated the craftsmanship of the dwarves. She never involved herself with their society, as they often hated all other races and treated her no differently. Despite her strength, she had little pride—she cared little for power or status. Instead, she spent her time learning from different races and observing their daily lives.

After thousands of years, she often felt lost. She experienced the pain of losing friends, including her dragon companion, who eventually disappeared. To cope, she turned to art. She began drawing in her human form, infusing magic into her illustrations. These drawings became her way of telling her tales and sharing her experiences, overcoming language barriers and allowing others to understand her story.

Her stories gained a new purpose when she met a sick elf child. The child discovered one of her magical books, which depicted healing and new life. The book helped the child recover, and she realized the incredible potential of her stories. She began experimenting, using her magic-infused tales to create life, solve hunger, and bring hope.

However, despite her efforts, she could not prevent her world from collapsing due to war and destruction. The planet was eventually destroyed, leaving her helpless to save everyone. Heartbroken, she retreated into her stories, hiding away from the chaos.

One day, a mysterious figure appeared—an enigmatic mystic, who offered her a chance at new life. The world she knew had been abandoned by the gods, and this mystic’s offer was a way to escape the dying planet. She accepted and vanished from her world forever, leaving her stories behind as her legacy to a dying world.
