### Actual Story

After being away from home for several years, she found the door lying busted on the ground, the heavy smell of blood thick in the air.

"Mom?! Dad?!" Her face paled, and her heart skipped a beat.

Growling and snarling echoed from within the house. With her heart in her throat, she quickly bolted inside to find her parents being eaten alive by two ugly, blood-soaked, two-headed beasts.

Having run in and shouted, she had gotten the attention of the two beasts. They slowly turned towards her, snarling and dripping blood from their mouths. She turned and bolted into the local park as quickly as she could.

Tears streamed down her face as she began to scream bloody murder for help, but to no avail; the surroundings seemed eerily quiet. Even in her sorrow, she had sensed something was wrong with the neighborhood, but now wasn't the time to focus on that. She turned her gaze backward to check if the monsters were following her.

Seeing one of the beasts following in hot pursuit, and the other stopping at the doorway before quickly disappearing from sight, she stopped in the center of the park field. She braced herself, wiping away her tears as she puffed up her chest, letting out an echoing scream. "Why did you have to kill them!? They were my family! My Mother, My Father!" Her mind raced with images of burning the monstrosity to the ground, with flames erupting from her hands, and the head of the monster in her hands.

Then, her once cold, pale hands came to life with blazing, blue-tinted flames.

Feeling the flames spark to life in her hands, she lunged at the beast's faces and plunged them into its coarse, brown fur-covered face, attempting to burn its face to cinders.

The beast, in a frenzied panic, slammed its whole body into the girl, knocking her aside and digging its sharp, blood-soaked fangs into her shoulders. She screamed in agonizing pain at the sensation of the two-headed dog's bite.

Almost as quickly as the intense pain hit her, it subsided. With a burst of strength, she grabbed the snout of one of the two heads and squeezed it with all her might. With a combination of desperate strength and the heat from the searing flames, she managed to pry the top jaw off, causing it to sear her hand. The left head had clamped harder, shaking its head, trying to tear the muscles in her shoulder. Unfazed, she repeated her actions on the left head, causing the beast's grip to cease entirely. Pulling herself together for a moment, she took a deep breath in, and then out. She began to think to herself, "I have slayed this dog? But can I do it to the other..."

She pulled herself together, took a deep breath, and began to collect herself. The flames on her hands had already extinguished. She looked over her wounds; her shoulders had deep gouges, and blood dripped down them. She took her shirt off and began ripping it up to hold her wounds closed. "Luckily, it wasn't too deep." She marched forward, back towards her parents' home. She sneaked up on the house this time, and peered her head around to check if the beast was still there, feasting on her parents.

The beast stood there, enjoying its feast. Seeing this, she focused her thoughts on the flame from before and imagined it shooting out at the beast, that was about fifteen feet away from her now. Flames shot from her hands at the beast, setting it ablaze as it came towards her, in a frenzy. As the beast ran at her, she slid a small dagger out of its sheath on her thigh and held it firm, pointed at the raging beast's head. The beast slammed into her and knocked her down. As the dagger penetrated the skull of the charging creature, it collapsed on the ground, only mere feet away from her.

She looked to the beast and screamed loudly, tears flowing down her face.

Not even a single word came out; she remained there crying for some time before closing her eyes and falling backward. "Mom, I'm sorry. I'm home now. Dad, I'm sorry we never got to play catch."
