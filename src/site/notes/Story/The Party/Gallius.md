# Backstory
Among the winding groves of the Feywild—where moonlight drips like silver through endless leaves and time bends like a reed in the wind—Gallius was born beneath the ancient boughs of a centaur clan sworn to one of the court’s lesser royal houses. The Feywild is a place where beauty and terror live side by side, and Gallius learned early that strength alone was not enough to survive its shifting politics. Honor, loyalty, and bargains carried more weight than steel.
Gallius was not born into a world filled with love, he was born with a purpose. Bred by the finest warriors with the ambition of defending. If he ever failed at these simple goals he would be struck down as a mere child for failing.

Gallius was never the most graceful of his kin. While others mastered the bow or danced through the forest like spirits, Gallius fought with his fists. He believed in the honesty of a strike—the clarity of strength meeting strength. It was this stubborn devotion that caught the attention of a royal patron of his clan, a being older than memory and far stranger than any mortal creature.

When Gallius came of age, he was summoned before the royal court. There, beneath glowing mushrooms and whispering trees, he was offered a bargain.

Power.

Not the kind granted to knights or priests—but something deeper. Something older. In exchange for loyalty and service to the royal house, Gallius would be gifted a fragment of their otherworldly essence. He accepted without hesitation.

The magic changed him.

Where once his body moved like that of a proud centaur warrior, the fey power twisted and reshaped him. His body would morph in unnatural ways turning himself into more of a beast than any other in battle had ever seen. In battle, his body could warp and contort into shapes that unsettled even hardened fighters—limbs moving in impossible arcs, blows striking from angles no opponent expected.

To the Fey, this transformation was simply the nature of their magic.

But Gallius was not destined to remain in the Feywild forever.

Whether sent as an agent of his royal patron, exiled by courtly intrigue, or simply cast into the mortal world through the strange currents of fey magic, Gallius eventually found himself far from the twisting forests of his birth. His reason for being exiled completely lost to him in the process of being sent to the mortal realm. Being torn down and finding himself unable to use his previous otherworldly abilities, for now. The mortal realm was harsher in its own way. There were no ancient courts or enchanted groves—only crowded streets and the constant need to survive. Surrounded by humans who saw him as an outcast for what they fey was doing to their world, slowly corrupting and tearing into it. He did not understand why or how he was seen in this light, all he knew was he must persevere.

So Gallius did what he had always done.

He fought.

In the back alleys and underground rings of the cities, the centaur pugilist quickly earned a reputation. Opponents expected brute strength from a creature his size. They did not expect the shear mastery and ability of someone who had spent as much time as him training. Blow after blow fell with brutal precision until challengers learned to fear the quiet centaur who never spoke during a match.

Yet Gallius fights for more than coin.

Somewhere beyond the mortal sky lies the Feywild, where time still flows strangely and the royal patron who marked him with power still watches. Every fight, every battle, every step Gallius takes in this world is part of a path laid before him by a bargain older than most kingdoms.

And one day, when the time is right, the court of the Fey will call upon him again. Or something far deeper may call, coming from the power he holds within.

When that day comes, Gallius will answer.

Because a promise made in the Feywild is never truly forgotten.
