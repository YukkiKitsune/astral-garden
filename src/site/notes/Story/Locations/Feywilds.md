### The Fae
What you've heard is probably a lot of seelie propaganda. People would have you believe the seelie are good and the unseelie are bad, but this couldn't be further from the truth. The unseelie and seelie are like yin and yang, night and day, one is darker yes, but neither are good or evil.

The seelie/unseelie courts are the highest and largest courts, each containing smaller courts within: the seelie have the summer and spring courts, the unseelie have the winter and autumn courts, and then there's a bunch of lesser-known courts within the two as well.

The major difference is that the unseelie are darker-enclined, you'll find fae like the dullahan, bean sidhe, and many changelings belong to the unseelie court. The "creepy" fae tend to be unseelie, but despite this most are misunderstood or misrepresented in popular culture and don't actually do harm or have evil intent. Take the bean sidhe - now known as the banshee - a fae which comes as a warning of death and usually helps people grieving but doesn't actually cause the death, yet pop culture has turned it into a wailing vengeful spirit.

Meanwhile the seelie house fae like brownies, sprites, and pixies. They get the good reputation because their style of fae are easy to market as happy, fun, and helpful. These are the ones you'll find turned into fairy godmothers and tinkerbell in disney movies. In reality, they're just as tricksy if not more so than the unseelie and the consequences of their deals can be disastrous. They value their own entertainment over the lives of humans.

In terms of behavior, it varies from fae to fae, but generally the unseelie respect some form of order, follow closer to logic, are more calm and collected, introverted, and our deals are usually fairly and clearly stated even if we leave room for loopholes and double entendre, like signing a contract or making a verbal agreement. Think of the unseelie as lawful neutral.

The seelie are more chaotic, wild, creative, impulsive, and extroverted. They do things less for a reason and more because they want to, and their deals are usually vague and don't set clear terms, like "I gave you something so now you owe me whatever I want of equal value". The seelie are more like chaotic neutral.

Course, all this varies and not all fae are the same even within the same courts, and the smaller courts also present their own differences. This is just a broad overview.
