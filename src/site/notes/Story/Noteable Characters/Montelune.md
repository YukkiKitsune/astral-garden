# Appearance
Encased in gleaming silver plate trimmed with midnight black, you cut a striking figure against any backdrop. Faint blue enamel etchings wind across your armor like frozen lightning, matching the icy glint in your pale eyes. Silver hair spills from beneath your helm, a shimmering banner against your deep obsidian skin. Every step sends a muted ripple of reflected moonlight along your polished armor — a moving statue of steel and shadow, built for the night’s crusade.
# History
"The Moon’s Last Mistake" I was born in the deepest black of Gravespiral Hollow, where even the air tastes of cold iron and despair… and, faintly, fermented mushroom beer. The midwife said I didn’t cry — I sang, a high, haunting note that made her knock over her mug of spider‑milk. I was the eighteenth unwanted accident of my mother, Matron Vlyssithe, Bride of the Blade, and my father, Drath’vel, the Accountant Who Counts Only Deaths. My earliest memory is standing in our crumbling obsidian hall, holding a dagger too big for my hands, cutting my palm just to feel something real. Childhood was a blur of polishing ceremonial murder‑spoons and reciting the Forty‑Seven Forbidden Lullabies until my tongue bled. Then came the night of the Festival of Screams, when the sacred spiderweb bungee collapsed and crushed my grandmother’s mushroom wine cellar. The entire clan was ruined — not by war, but by a drunken gelatinous cube that “just wanted to dance.” It was then I swore an oath: No more joy. Only vengeance. I prayed to the goddess Eilistrae — but my prayers were swallowed by the wrong cosmos. Instead, Nocturnalia herself whispered through the darkness: “Rise, my child… and make every night bleed with meaning.” And so I rose. I forged my sacred weapon: The Moonpiercer, a longsword with a pommel shaped like a crying moon. I wrapped myself in chainmail stitched from the melted anklets of my fallen cousins, every link a name, every name a curse. I became a blade in the dark… sworn to rid the world of cowards, liars, and those who eat the last piece of cake without asking. Some call me a champion. Others, a lunatic. But I… I am The Last Mistake the Moon Ever Made. And when I die, I will drag the stars down into the dirt with me.
