# Appearance
A petite, energetic desert elf with sun-warmed tan skin. Their face is perpetually bent into an amused smirk. Ruby eyes glance past you, stopping briefly on a  whim. Their hair is a straight, long jet of black going down their back. Faint burn marks, scars, and oil smudges cover random splotches of their body like constellations. The air around them smells like an odd mix of oil and sand.

Their fingers constantly fidget as restless energy funnels through them causing them to be an endless cyclone of energy.
# Backstory
Modern Day, leading up to the Tower

One splash at a time, purple paint adheres itself across the empty arena walls. A strange, ticking, metallic device is found to be the source of this graffiti monstrosity, firing away near a cheery, laughing desert elf satisfied with her latest invention. As each glob of paint is fired towards another structure within the arena, a messy line drips down onto the floor again and again…

Then comes the day of the next fight: paint exchanged for hot, iron bullets, Acacia sets what appeared to be just another one of her pranks down onto the arena floor. “This should be fun~! After that stupid gnome made fun of me they were really asking for it today. There’s no way they’ll be ready for this! I spent days making all these special bullets!” On queue, an average, scruffy gnome enters the arena with their machine, “The Bronze Pummeler.” Befitting its name, the unmistakable shine of bronze coats the machine from each mechanical finger down to the very rims holding the wheels together. Acacia announces her machine with her normal, awful naming sense, “Pera Mobile Rifle!” The Pera Mobile Rifle is a dark, iron machine featuring two turrets mounted on a set of wheels, and a small screen that displays various, cute facial expressions.

The fearsome, draconic owner of the arena, Y’sil, announced the start of the match. 

The Bronze Pummeler immediately dashes towards the Pera Mobile Rifle, and the first barrage of bullets fire away at it. Hereon simply referred to as “Pera,” attempts to keep a line of fire going while driving away from the Bronze Pummeler. However, the distance between the two is closing, and Pera was not made for close range combat. Acacia inputs a new command as she glances at the gnome’s sluggish, apathetic face. It’s as if he feels he can win using one strategy alone, befitting a newcomer to the Y’sil Clockwork Arena. As soon as the command is transmitted, Pera fires at the ground. “Ha! Are you stupid! Your machine can’t even aim properly!” shouts Acacia’s adversary. The riled up crown transitions from cheering to laughter. Then, looking closer, the gnome notices the bullet casings are opening up onto the arena floor, spilling gunpowder onto the ground. Ordinarily, this would be a continuation of what appears to be a drastic failure. However, the smirk on Acacia’s face hints otherwise. This is no ordinary gunpowder…. the paint graffitied around the bullet casings because to burn. “Did you just think it was another usual prank, stupid gnome? No, this paint burns easily, and it burns HOT! Prepare yourself! You went with classic bronze on your machine! As you know, Bronze melts at 1880 degrees! Meanwhile, I built of Iron…. Needing a few hundred more degrees to melt! Now here’s a question for you, what temperature do you think the paint is burning at?” Acacia laughs as she watches the gnome’s horrified face. The rubber wheels on both machines begin to break down in the ever-spreading flame, locking The Bronze Pummeler and Pera in place. Acacia places her next command, “Fire directly at the fool!” and with the whirring of the gears, both turrets redirect and unleash a barrage at the slowly melting machine. Unable to bear seeing his creation melt any further, the gnome forfeits. Y’sil unleashes a light dust of magic which floats up to the top of the arena, and lets loose a torrent confined to the arena before he  declares Acacia’s 54th victory of this year.

This was just the average week for Acacia. Tinkering away at her inventions, thinking of different ways to pull pranks, and guiding her inventions in the arena. Those who live with her at the arena have gotten used to expecting various new antics, both in and out of battle. However, they never feel quite prepared for the newest prank. Originally, Acacia was a simple wanderer. She travelled from place to place seeing new sights, meeting new people, and learning many new things. She scrounged up various metals and dismantled abandoned items to create new items to aid herself in her travels, until one day within the  Migiai Desert came… 

It was originally like an other ordinary day, Acacia prepared a simple meal, to stock of her supplies, and set off in whatever direction felt right at the moment. But what she wasn’t expecting in the middle of nowhere was the monstrous colosseum. There was no way she could pass this place up. She immediately entered and was enticed to find the owner was no mere mortal, but a blue dragon! Surprised at her surroundings she discovered the art of the Clockfighters – a place inventors gather to create special clockwork machines to fight against each other. Acacia started her first matches and felt unprecedented glee, and resolved to spend at least a few decades enjoying the art. 

Year after year passed, until Acacia got word of a mysterious tower being discovered on the lands. Her adventurous spirit felt a tug, and she was satisfied with her long list of achievements at the arena. Acacia decided to set off and explore this tower people are making a fuss over, and would not rest until her thirst for discovery is sated once more.

140 years before the Clockwork Arena

Acacia was new to adventuring and finally leaving her little unnamed village for the first time. Located near an oasis in the grand desert of Sari’fa, it had plenty of food and water supply coming in, but life sure wasn’t easy. And as the years went by, Acacia’s desire to explore the world only increased in magnitude. They had little need for coin in their little village, and so Acacia felt dumbstruck when she came to resupply at the first town she came across, a two month trek away from home. She stumbled into a board full of odd-jobs, from helping repair homes and escort missions to full-on extermination missions. The extermination missions didn’t end at mere pests, but could even escalate to bandit camps full of people. Acacia knew she was far underequipped for such missions, but had no gripes about them otherwise. She spent a few weeks taking odd jobs and hunting for food when she eventually came across a band of six mercenaries of assorted races consisting of a goblin, dwarf, desert elf, two dragonborn brothers, and a halfling. They introduced themselves as “Gehk, Ironhand, Safi, Drulthun, Clatish, and Greg” respectively. To which Acacia responded “pretty small mercenary group you have going on!” The goblin, dwarf, and halfling stared daggers at her for a moment as she obliviously continued on. “I’ve been taking odd jobs for several weeks now. It’s helped me out a bit but it’s difficult to acquire better equipment only helping people deliver small items most days. Escort missions pay well but there’s so few.. but together we could take on some of the extermination missions! Would you consider letting me join your Mercenary group for a while?”

The hammerhead-for-a-hand dwarf, Ironhand, neutrally responded, “well, we could always use more hands – no pun intended – but how do we know you’re really up to the task? How do we know you aren’t just going to stab us in the back and take the entire reward for yourself?”

Acacia pondered for a moment in deep thought. With plenty of energy she cheered, “The only way you’ll find out if I’m good enough is by coming out into the field and seeing for yourself! As for stabbing you all in the back…. there’s six of you! SIX! There’s no way I could take you all on at the same time. If anything, I should be the one worried about being stabbed in the back. However, we’re in the middle of the desert. This isn’t even a well populated town, only perhaps a thousand people live here! If either of us were to betray each other, word would travel fast. And a ruined reputation makes for a ruined mercenary! Therefore it’d utterly foolish for either of us to cause problems. I know you probably don’t want to split money with a seventh person, but consider the jobs we could take!” Acacia then excitedly points at a weathered down posting on the board, her eyes glimmering. “This bandit camp job has been here ever since I arrived in this town. Week after week, no one took it, including your group. It pays well, but it clearly must be too dangerous for anyone at the moment to want to try it. But if you had another member, suddenly the odds aren’t so unbalanced. 12 bandits, 7 of us. It’ll take some strategizing, but if we watch them closely and watch their patrols, we could ambush them and then quickly overwhelm those who are resting at the camp. If we’re lucky we can move quickly enough to stop the sentry from waking anyone up, I have as much faith in my Chakram as I do anything. And if it doesn’t go so well…” Acacia pauses and makes an odd expression befitting her previously wholesome excitement: lust. Her face flushed, she declares, “we always have the option of blood-bathing them while they’re awake.” 

Greg the halfling’s confidence rose steadily as Acacia spoke, and then dropped into an out of place, confused feeling. “Erm… oddly excited you seem at the prospect that we can’t kill them in their sleep. But a bit of crazy isn’t bad in a merc.”

Drulthun announces, “okay. I say you have a fine point. Since you’re just starting out with us, we’ll take a few of the lesser extermination missions first. But if you hold your weight well, and I mean well, you can stay with us. Clatish?”

“Agreed. We’ll start off with the desert mice extermination, a nest has been discovered only a quarter day’s walk from town, but their numbers have been getting out of hand. There’s even rumors of something odd going on, and some of the mice the guards found in town even have light traces of magic. My bet is some deluded fool is attempting to create a small mouse empire” Clatish stated with the full confidence of someone who isn’t attributing a creature infestation to an utterly stupid idea. “By the way, me and Drulthun are the leaders of the group if that wasn’t obvious. We founded this corps together, and so we stick together. Learn from us and stay close to the group unless ordered to scout ahead or make a diversion, understood? That means in town too. Today’s the only exception, that way you can get acclimated and move your belongings over. We’ll even help as a show of good faith, but only after this first mission.”

Acacia agreed to their terms and so the party headed outwards to their first mission as a group of seven. 

As the group made the small journey to the mouse nest, Acacia excitedly talked on and on with her new peers. They hardly felt room to respond – as if she could be sated with a conversation with herself. She just kept talking and talking, until eventually Safi spoke up and brought in stories of their own, and the two felt a small kinship connecting them as if all the elves in this desert had the same childhood, even if the local lore varies a little. Eventually, the nest came into eyeshot. 

“What in the hell do you reckin’ that is Packmul- er, Clatish?” stated the stinky little goblin, Gehk. 

“I’ve never seen anything quite like it… the mice… can use magic? It’s as if they have their own sorcerers, infusing the others with magic. I’ve got a bad feeling about this.”

Acacia chimes in, “but it’s kind of cute. The widdle mice learned how to cast. Are they going to fire cute widdle mouse-sized magic bolts too?”

Ironhand bets 40 Silver on exploding mice, while Greg makes a disgusted face and checks over his equipment. 

“I sure hope not. Even if the magic bolts are mouse-sized, magic is still magic. It’ll be dangerous if we get hit. We need to take out the casters first before the infuse any more mice. And account for some of the mice not being home too, it might be easier if we setup a food supply for them nearby – but poison it. We are not getting bitten by hundreds of magic-casting vermin today. Clatish, unload some of our food supplies right here. Gehk, you’re in charge of formulating the poison and tainting our supplies. With any luck this will wipe out the casters as well. We’ll setup a temporary camp just over the hill and keep an eye on things – it’ll make tomorrow much easier. The rest of you get started on said camp, dusk will be approaching us soon. Usually one of us is on night watch but on account of Acacia being new, you and Ironhand take first watch once camp is ready.”

And just like that, everyone accepted Drulthun’s orders and got to work. Gehk combined grease, soda, partially spoiled hot dogs, and a few other mystery ingredients in a vial while excitedly muttering, “you know it’s a good poison when it becomes a glowing murky green, don’ worry ‘bout it Greg. Why you watchin’ anyway? You wanna test it? Here, give it a smell!” Gehk shoves the vial under Greg’s nose, which regrettably vomits into the vial. “Perfect, the missing ingredient – halfling virgin puke” every merc in earshot made a concerned glance toward the goblin, but dismissed his actions on account of being his group status as “slightly better sewer rat.” Greg complains about both the virgin comment and smelling Gehk’s mix without consent, to which he is completely ignored. Ironhand hurls a rock scarily close to where Greg is standing, as if saying “help us set up camp or that won’t be the last thing you regret.” 

In the early hours of the morning, Safi was on watch. He ran panicked into the tents and woke everyone up, as a familiar spell rained down nearby… “FIREBALL! MOVE OUT OF THE WAY!” Everyone staggered out of the tents confused, seeing a small group of angry magic-swinging mice casting a rain of hellfire. Then, a mice dressed in an ornate looking saddle proclaimed, “YOU WHO WOULD DO US HARM. SLAUGHTERER OF HUNDREDS. YOU WILL PAY. EVERY PLAGUE BRINGER WILL BE BROUGHT UNDER FOOT!” 

Acacia looked dumbfounded at this small regal vermin. “Y-you can talk?!”

“OF COURSE YOU HUMANS AND YOUR ASSUMPTIONS. YOU USE SPELLS AND POTIONS TO SPEAK TO ANIMALS… BUT CONSIDER THIS SPELL – HUMANOID SPEAKING! A CULTIVATION OF MY OWN, I HOPED TO FORGE AN ALLIANCE WITH THE GREAT ONES, THOSE WHO FEED US WITH THEIR MANY CRUMBS. HOWEVER, YOU FEW HAVE COME TO WIPE US OUT, AND FOR THAT YOU SHALL PAY! LET THERE BE NO MORE WORDS AS YOU REGRETA-“ the mice’s head explodes into a small fountain of blood. The gleam of a metal chakram cuts its way through the air, taking out four casters before finally cementing itself into the ground. “Nobody has time for you to run your mouth! Everyone, let’s take them down!” Acacia exclaims as she tosses her second chakram out. She gets visibly more excited as more mice perish in a variety of ways, however, despite the few dozen casters that showed up to the battle, they were still small in stature – and so the sands were only marginally stained with mouse blood. By the time they finished, the party was moderately annoyed their sleep, meditations, and other night time activities were interrupted by these vermin. They found the nest filled with corpses – the poison was as potent as hoped and wiped them out fairly quickly. 

With the nest extinguished, the party packed up their camp after a few hours of various recreational activities, and cashed in their reward in town. “we meant to move your belongings to our more permanent camp yesterday, but that nest was bigger and… stranger than we expected.” Clatish seemed a little distracted, but kept talking nonetheless. ”It got us a little extra pay, but today we’ll move your belongings before we take on our next assignment, Acacia.”  
“No, need to worry. I don’t have much, all my belongings are on me right now. I sold off some old things to help afford better equipment, everything I own is on me right now. I never need to sleep so I don’t feel the need of a tent when there’s so much to do instead! If I had more stuff it would only clutter my adventures anyway.”  
“Oh alright… well, we were wondering something, why exactly do you appear to… get excited when you see or think about things being killed?”

Acacia laughs a little. “I noticed you people don’t. It’s a little weird, honestly. Where I come from, everyone gets excited from that. Even when we hunt for food, we try our best to make the animal’s organs explode everywhere, even if it makes it harder to gather things together to eat later. It saves a little time on butchering, though. The taste of blood is exhilarating, and doing things like that feels very intimate with my target ♥. It’s in our culture, you could say, much like how it’s in your culture to… um… not do that. It’s okay though, your weird ways of not being effected by these things don’t bother me!” 

Clatish looks very concerned, and quietly resolves to never, ever, visit this village. To that end he asks her where it is, and finds himself dismayed at the thought that he shares the desert with such a place. “I don’t think that’s the problem Acacia… but you do you.” Acacia gets a little turned on and overwhelms him with various bloody stories as the party heads to their permanent camp. 

The group completed various assignments over the coming days until they were finally satisfied with the test run of letting Acacia on-board. They considered a small celebration, but decided to hold it off until the bandit camp assignment she first proposed is completed – at which point they could have an even larger celebration. Acacia got herself extra Chakram and a few other pieces of equipment as she gained more gold from each assignment, fully satisfied. And then finally, the day came to take on the bandit camp.
